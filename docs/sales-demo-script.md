# CaseLoad Screen — Sales Demo Script

**Audience:** Toronto-based sole practitioner or 2-lawyer firm partner. Likely already running Google Ads, frustrated with intake quality.

**Length:** 8-12 minutes including the live demo.

**Demo URL:** https://app.caseloadselect.ca/widget-v2/demo/[firmId]

**Setup before the call:**
- Open the demo URL in Chrome on the largest screen available (27" or external monitor preferred).
- Use the Hartwell Law DEMO firm ID for first-time pitches: `1f5a2391-85d8-45a2-b427-90441e78a93c`.
- Have the prospect's actual practice area in mind (PI, employment, immigration, family) so you can demo a scenario relevant to them.
- Hide your bookmarks bar. Maximise the browser window. Disable notifications.

## The 60-second cold open

Lead with the problem before the product.

> "Every law firm I work with says the same thing: the leads keep coming, but most of them are wrong. The $100,000 case and the tire-kicker arrive the same way — same form, same call, same minute of staff time. By the time you figure out which is which, you've burned half a day."
>
> "What if your intake decided that for you, before anyone on your team picked up the phone? That's what CaseLoad Screen is. Let me show you."

Switch to the demo URL. Don't explain the layout yet — let them see it.

## The walkthrough (5 minutes, voice-over while you click)

### Open

Point at the right side first, briefly.

> "On the right is what your operator and the lawyer see. Live scoring as the prospect fills out the intake. We'll come back to it."
>
> "On the left is what the prospect sees on their phone or your website."

### Step 1 — Kickoff (40 seconds)

In the textarea on the left, type a scenario realistic for the prospect's practice area. Pick one:

| Practice area | Scenario to type |
|---|---|
| PI | "I was rear-ended on the QEW two weeks ago and I still have neck pain" |
| Employment | "I was let go from my finance director job last month after 8 years, no reason given" |
| Family | "My ex-spouse stopped the parenting time arrangement we had and won't respond to my emails" |
| Immigration | "My partner and I want to start a spousal sponsorship application" |

> "Imagine this is a prospective client landing on your website. Two sentences, voice or text — same as a phone call would be."

Click **Continue**.

### Step 2 — AI thinking (3 seconds)

> "The AI reads what they wrote, classifies the practice area and sub-type, and pulls the right question set. Watch the right panel."

Pause. Let the score animate up. Let the band badge appear.

> "Already you can see this is — [Band B / personal injury / motor vehicle accident]. Real-time. Before any human touched it."

### Step 3 — Round 1 questions (1 minute)

Tap through the cards. Three things to point out:

> 1. "One question per screen, big tap targets, no typing. This is what works on mobile, where 70% of legal traffic lives."
> 2. "Every option has a scoring weight set by a lawyer — not by us. The AI doesn't decide if a case is worth your time. The lawyer's rules do."
> 3. "Watch the score on the right. Every tap, the score moves. Every tap, the operator sees what changed."

Tap something that triggers a meaningful score jump. Let the prospect see the score climb.

### Step 4 — Round 2 (45 seconds)

After the second AI thinking transition:

> "Round 2 goes deeper into the case strength. By now we know the practice area, the timing, the rough merit. The system is asking the questions a senior lawyer would ask in a phone screen — not generic intake questions."

### Step 5 — Identity + OTP (30 seconds)

Fast-forward through name, email, phone. For OTP, type any 6 digits (demo bypass).

> "OTP is the cutoff. Anyone serious about retaining a lawyer will verify their phone. Anyone who won't — that's a tire-kicker filtering themselves out, before your team has spent any time on them."

### Step 6 — Round 3 (1 minute)

> "Now we're past OTP. This prospect has skin in the game. Round 3 asks the deep questions that turn into your case file — the same questions a lawyer would dig into during the consultation. But now you've got them in writing, before the consultation starts."

Tap through 3-4 R3 cards.

### Step 7 — Done state (30 seconds)

When the Done screen appears on the left, focus the prospect's attention on the right.

> "Final case file. Band [A/B], CPI [score]/100, recommended next action: [whatever it says]."
>
> "Your operator opens this in the portal. Lawyer reads the memo we generated. They walk into the consultation already knowing the case. Conversion goes up because the lawyer isn't doing intake — they're closing."

## Common objections + responses

**"How is this different from Lawmatics or Clio Grow?"**
> "Lawmatics and Clio Grow are CRMs — they file leads after they arrive. CaseLoad Screen filters leads before they arrive. Different problem. We replace the intake form, not the CRM. Most of our firms keep Clio Manage and use us upstream of it."

**"What about LSO compliance?"**
> "Every question is reviewed against Rule 4.2-1. No outcome promises, no specialist language, no superlatives. The system is more conservative than most firms' own websites."

**"Will it work for my practice area?"**
> "We have question banks for 35 practice areas across 13 umbrellas. If you do anything in personal injury, employment, family, immigration, or civil litigation, it's already authored. For niche areas, I author a custom bank during onboarding — usually 2-3 hours of your time over a week."

**"How long does setup take?"**
> "Two weeks from contract signature to live on your website. Most of that is waiting for your DNS provider — actual configuration is one day. Onboarding includes Clio OAuth, conflict register import, branding, practice area config, and a Discovery call to tune the question weights."

**"What if a lead's answer is 'other' — does the system break?"**
> "Every question has an Other → free-text option. If the lead's situation doesn't match the structured options, they explain in their own words. The lawyer sees that text in the memo. The system doesn't dead-end on edge cases."

**"My existing intake form converts at X%. Will this beat that?"**
> "The 2026 industry benchmark for legal landing-page conversion is 17.6% for firms with interactive multi-step intake. Static contact forms sit at 2-3%. Most law firm sites are at 4-7%. The split-screen demo you just saw isn't speculative — it's the same engine that's running in production today."

## What NOT to say

- Do not call it "AI" more than twice. Most prospects are AI-fatigued. Lead with the *outcome* (filter, score, route), not the technology.
- Do not promise specific conversion lifts. Use the industry benchmark. Promise the *method*, not the *result*.
- Do not mention competitors by name unless asked.
- Do not show the operator dashboard or admin pipeline in the first call. That's a follow-up call. The first call is just the filter.
- Do not use any of the AI vocabulary banned in the brand guide ("seamlessly", "delve", "tapestry", etc.)
