DRG · Law · The Counsel Letter
On choosing the practice

Why this firm reads differently·

By Damaris Regina Guimaraes · Founder, DRG Law

Most owners learn the hard way that legal counsel only becomes business clarity when one lawyer carries the matter from start to finish. At DRG that is not a marketing line, it is how every file is structured, written, and closed. The six commitments below describe the practice as it is actually run, not how it is pitched.

No. 01 · The Difference

Owner-decision framing·

Every matter begins with the business decision the legal task must support. Incorporation, lease, partner change, sale, succession. The law is framed around the move, not the move around the law. The brief names the decision before it names a document.

No. 02 · The Difference

Same-lawyer continuity·

The lawyer who reads the intake is the lawyer who closes the matter. There are no associates to hand off to. One file, one hand, from the first call to the last signature.

No. 03 · The Difference

Counsel in the language you think in·

English or Portuguese, at the same structural depth. Portuguese is not a translation laid over advice given in English; it carries the same legal precision. Decision-quality in either language.

Legal clarity protects the decisions that build the business.
No. 04 · The Discipline

Business goal first·

The first sentence of every meeting names the business move, not the legal task. Statutes and documents appear only after the goal is on the page. Every brief in this office opens the same way.

No. 05 · The Discipline

Clarity before commitment·

Risk, price, timing, decision, and next step are named in writing before the owner signs. No vague timing, no surprise fee logic, no advice that becomes clear only after the commitment has already been made.

No. 06 · The Discipline

Protected, not patronized·

Tradeoffs are explained plainly so the owner can decide with full agency. Calm, adult, precise. The brief leaves the client capable of choosing, never managed into the result the lawyer would have picked.

Note for the strategic call · 2026-05-27

Three different reading experiences of the same six commitments.

Each territory expresses the same brand doctrine — legal clarity protects the decisions that build the business — but produces a different homepage experience. Counsel: editorial cover plus founder's column. Wayfinder: system view plus literal route. Practice: contemporary product page plus asymmetric bento grid.

The three options trade off on different things. Counsel is highest editorial discipline; every piece needs writing. Wayfinder requires the route metaphor to stay coherent end to end. Practice is the most operationally light; one accent, one period, one type family.
