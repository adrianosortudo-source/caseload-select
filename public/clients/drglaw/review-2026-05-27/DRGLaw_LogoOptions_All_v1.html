DRG Law · 9 Logo Options · Master Sheet

Counsel · Wayfinder · Practice · v1 · 2026-05-27
Three visual territories, three concepts per territory, nine logo + icon options total. Each territory carries its native typography, palette, and signature discipline. Each option ships the same three roles: primary wordmark for hero use, founder lockup for correspondence and About, monogram for favicon and seal. Icons render on three grounds with a 96px → 16px scale test. Comparison matrix at the end.

The Counsel.

Territory 01 of 03 · Source Serif 4 · Oxblood + brass + cream
Editorial founder-led identity. The mark must read as a counselor's office and a writer's column before it reads as a brand. All three concepts use Source Serif 4 for the wordmark and reserve oxblood as the only saturated colour, with brass for rule work.
A
Concept A · The middle dot

A held breath between two words.

The locked v8.5 signature. Oxblood middle-dot binds DRG to Law in the wordmark, recurs in the masthead and byline, and works as the standalone icon — a single mark of attention. Closest to the Sunday-column register the brand book describes.

Construction
Middle dot is the typographic middle-dot character (U+00B7) as an oxblood tspan inside the wordmark. Inherits x-height position from Source Serif 4 directly. Framed icon tiles render the full wordmark in a brass border; the cream tile carries the standalone dot as the pure mark.
Signature deployment
Wordmark + masthead + at least one inline use per page. Never a bullet, never decoration, never substituted for period or comma.
Risk
At very small sizes the dot can read as a stray pixel. The icon-only mark uses a 46-radius dot in the 500-grid (≈9% width) to stay legible at 16px.

The Wayfinder →

Territory 02 of 03 · Space Grotesk + Inter + JetBrains Mono · Blue + amber
Architectural decision-map identity. The mark must read as navigation infrastructure before it reads as a law firm. All three concepts run on Space Grotesk display, Inter body, JetBrains Mono for coordinate data, deep blue as the structural ground, and signal amber as the only saturated colour.
A
Concept A · The arrow

The route forward, written in.

The locked v8.5 signature. An amber arrow points from DRG to Law in the wordmark itself, and recurs across every wayfinding surface as the marker of motion. The mark is the firm's promise made visual: every matter has a next step, and the firm knows what it is.

Construction
Arrow is geometric: shaft and head built from 36-stroke segments on the 500-grid. Head subtends 90° at the tip. No tapers, no curves. Same arrow used at every scale.
Signature deployment
Wordmark + at least one wayfinding use + at least one CTA arrow per page. Same arrow serves as section header, breadcrumb, list marker, status icon.
Risk
Arrow direction makes the mark non-symmetric — must always face the reading direction. For RTL languages the entire mark mirrors; never just the arrow.
B
Concept B · The station tag

The plate above the gate.

The mark IS a transit-sign rectangle. Navy enamel plate, amber edge accent acting as the platform stripe, two words sitting in two cells. Reads as wayfinding furniture before it reads as a wordmark — the highest "navigation infrastructure" register of the three options.

The Practice.

Territory 03 of 03 · Inter + JetBrains Mono · Ink + moss + sand
Contemporary professional services identity. The mark must read as the modern advisor's surface — Stripe Press, Linear, Pentagram NYC — before it reads as a law firm. All three concepts run on Inter at the wordmark and JetBrains Mono for metadata, with moss as the single chromatic note.

A closed decision.

The locked v8.5 signature. A single moss period closes the wordmark, every h1, and the founder lockup itself. The period is the moment a matter is signed, executed, decided — the doctrine made into a single mark of punctuation, used only at moments of closure.

Construction
Period is a moss-filled tspan inside the wordmark text element — inherits baseline, kerning, and optical position from Inter itself. No manual repositioning across sizes. Wordmark letter-spacing -0.02em.
Signature deployment
Wordmark + every h1/h2 closure + footer mark. Period appears only at moments of closure, never mid-sentence in moss. Roughly 3-5 periods per surface.
Risk
At 16px the period collapses against the DRG letterform. Solution: at favicon, the period takes the whole tile as a single moss circle (shown above).

Concept B · The moss block

The Stripe Press edition mark.

A heavy DRG set inside a moss square — the brand reference is Stripe Press editions and Pentagram's bookplate work. The block IS the mark; the wordmark trails it on the surface. The strongest territory-mark of the three, the most ownable, and the easiest to apply across product chrome.
