First-person voice · the founder manifesto

Why I start with the business decision

From Damaris · founder of DRG Law

I started this practice because the legal experience most owners get does not fit what they actually need. They get an oral opinion at the consult, a bill that surprises them, and paper at the closing table with no chance to read it carefully.

The owners I work with are not lawyers. They build businesses, sign leases, take on partners, transfer property. They do not need a legal lecture; they need the legal structure framed around the business decision they are trying to make. So that is what I do. I start with the business goal, explain the tradeoffs, and turn the legal complexity into a written structure the client can act on.

I work in English and Portuguese because most of the owners I serve switch to Portuguese when the legal point gets dense. Their decisions get made in Portuguese. My briefs follow them there.

I refuse to grow this firm by adding lawyers. Same-lawyer continuity is the promise. The owners who choose DRG choose me. I read every intake. I run every file. I close every matter.

If that is the lawyer you are looking for, we will work together well.

Damaris Regina Guimaraes · Founder, DRG Law Professional Corporation

The behavior model that guides tone, content, and design

Archetype

The archetype gives DRG a behavior hierarchy. It says what the brand should sound like, what it should never become, and how every surface should make the client feel.

Primary

Sage Authority

DRG clarifies, structures, cites, explains, and turns law into business decision-quality. The brand should lead with evidence, reasoning, and the ability to make complex legal choices understandable.

This is the first filter for every page, post, intake question, and visual system decision: does it make the owner smarter about the decision in front of them?

Secondary

Caregiver Warmth

Warmth protects the owner from signing, buying, leasing, transferring, or restructuring without understanding. It does not make the brand soft; it makes the advice feel safe to use.

Caregiver shows up through plain explanation, calm pacing, practical next steps, and the sense that Damaris is carrying responsibility with the client.

Personality

Calm Legal Translator

The personality is precise, adult, practical, and respectful. DRG translates legal consequence into owner language without reducing the work to friendliness, community signalling, or general legal help.

Guardrail: not aggressive fighter, luxury advisor, friendly generalist, or language-only community firm.

The beliefs that guide DRG behavior

Values

Values are the rules for how DRG behaves when advising, writing, designing intake, and deciding what content deserves to exist. They keep the brand useful to owners instead of turning it into a list of legal services.

Value 01

Business Goal First

DRG begins with what the owner is trying to make happen in the business: growth, purchase, lease, transfer, sale, financing, succession, or protection. The legal work is then framed around that business movement.

This keeps the firm from leading with statutes, documents, or process. The owner sees how the legal structure serves the outcome they came to protect.

In practice

Every page, intake, and meeting should answer: what business decision is this legal work helping the owner make?

Value 02

Clarity Before Commitment

Owners should know what they are taking on before they sign, transfer, close, finance, or restructure. DRG makes risk, price, timeline, decision, and next step clear early enough for the owner to act.

This value shapes the service experience: no vague timing, no hidden legal consequence, no surprise fee logic, no advice that only becomes clear after the commitment has already happened.

In practice

The owner leaves the conversation knowing what can go wrong, what it costs, when it happens, and what must be done next.

Value 03

Protected, Not Patronized

DRG protects owners by explaining tradeoffs plainly, not by taking control away from them. The tone is calm, adult, and precise. The owner should feel informed enough to decide, not handled.

This is especially important for Portuguese-connected clients who may be navigating Canadian legal systems in a second legal culture. Respect shows up through clarity, not overexplaining.

In practice

Copy and counsel should reduce pressure, preserve agency, and make the owner feel capable of choosing the next step.

The operating choices that make DRG hard to copy

Points of Difference

Points of Difference are the proof behind the positioning. They are not claims about being better; they are visible operating choices a competitor would have to copy in the way they run files.

POD 01

Owner-Decision Framing

DRG starts with the owner decision the legal task must support, then frames the legal structure around that decision. This shifts the conversation from paperwork to consequence.

The client is not asked to understand the law first. The lawyer translates the legal issue into the business choice the owner is actually making.

Visible proof

Practice pages, lead magnets, intake questions, and meeting briefs should name the owner decision before naming the document.

POD 02

Same-Lawyer Continuity

Damaris personally reads the intake, structures the matter, explains the tradeoffs, and stays responsible from first call to closing. One person carries the context, the risk, and the client relationship.

This matters because business and real estate decisions often create downstream consequences. Continuity prevents the client from retelling the story at each step.

Visible proof

Use the line: the lawyer who opens your file is the lawyer who closes it.

POD 03

EN / PT Legal Fluency

DRG can explain legal structure in the language the owner thinks in. Portuguese is treated as legal and business fluency, not as a translation feature added after the real advice happens in English.

The promise is not community marketing. It is decision quality across languages: the owner can understand the tradeoff deeply enough to sign, decline, renegotiate, or wait.

Visible proof

Use Portuguese as capability language: in English or Portuguese. Never let it replace the business-law positioning.

The Trust Segment at full depth

Target buyer profile

The fastest trust path is the Portuguese-connected SMB owner: where they come from, how they enter the category, and what has to feel true before they call.

Demographic
First or second-generation Portuguese-speaking immigrant from Brazil, Portugal, or Lusophone Africa. Aged 30-55. Owns a small or mid-sized business (1-25 employees) in the GTA. Earns C$100k-400k personally.
Linguistic pattern
Speaks Portuguese at home and in family business. Speaks English well enough for everyday work, but switches to Portuguese under stress or complexity. Reads legal English with caution; reads legal Portuguese with relief.
Referral source
Family member, accountant, broker, realtor, fellow business owner, or community contact. Almost never via Google search at first touch.
First behaviour
Asks the referring contact for the lawyer's name and number. Calls the firm before reading the website. Wants to hear a person, then sees the website to confirm legitimacy.
The fear
Signing something they do not fully understand. Missing a tax consequence. Losing a deal because a step was skipped. Being seen as unsophisticated by a Canadian-born counterparty.
Decision criteria

The consumer enemy

The consumer enemy
The moment of signing without understanding.

What this enemy costs the buyer

The buyer holds the pen above a contract, a lease, a closing document, or a corporate resolution, and does not know which clause matters, which date is binding, or what they have just agreed to. The signature happens because the room expects it, not because the buyer has chosen it.

The deal closes. Weeks later, a clause activates that they did not see. A lender deadline passes. A tax consequence appears. A partner asserts an interpretation the buyer never agreed to. The buyer feels stupid, then angry, then resigned. The next legal decision will be entered the same way, because no alternative was ever shown.

Four voice traits, one register

Speaking as DRG

The DRG voice carries five traits from the BOP. When a draft sounds wrong, identify which trait is missing or which off-archetype habit has crept in. Voice is recoverable through the matrix.

Trait
Description
Do
Don't
Plain English
Active voice. Short sentences. No jargon.
"You sign the lease on March 12." Calendar dates, named actor, named verb.
"Execution of the leasehold instrument is contemplated for mid-March." Passive, hedged, undated.
Evidence-Led
Names the risk. Explains the tradeoff. Cites the rule when useful. Shows the reasoning.
