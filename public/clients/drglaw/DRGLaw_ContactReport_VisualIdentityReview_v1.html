Three Territories on the Table

What was shown, and where Dâmaris landed.

Territory 01
The Counsel
Editorial · Founder-led
Sage-led territory built around clarity and considered authority. Serif typeface for headlines, oxblood as the colour anchor, a typographic medallion as signature element. Logo options included DG monogram, DG Law with circular dot, and DG Law inside horizontal rules. Reads as classy without ostentation. Closest in posture to an editorial publication.
Dâmaris’s leading choice
Territory 02
The Wayfinder
Architect of Decision
Directional territory. Arrow as signature element, navy and amber palette, blueprint grid background. Space Grotesk headlines, JetBrains Mono for metadata. Reads as confident and contemporary, with a startup register. Dâmaris noted it would suit a startup audience perfectly but pulls away from her preferred client profile.
Set aside
Territory 03
The Architect
Modern · Restrained
Most modern of the three. Terminal square as signature element, black and paper contrast, Inter as the typographic spine. Logo options included DG Law with terminal dot, DG in moss panel, and DG Law with complementary text above. Strongest Instagram application of the set. Dâmaris read it as classical in posture, second to Counsel on overall preference.
Runner-up
Decisions Recorded · Eight Items

What was decided in the room.

01 · Territory direction
The Counsel emerged as Dâmaris’s leading choice
Read as classy and sophisticated. Aligns with the ICP frame she stated in the room: fewer clients, willing to pay more, served at a higher register.
02 · Colour anchor
Oxblood called out as the standout colour
Dâmaris singled out the oxblood from Territory 1’s palette as the colour she liked most. Anchors the direction even before final lock-in.
03 · Logo preference
DG Law inside horizontal rules was the favoured mark
Of the three Counsel logo options, the horizontal-rule treatment won. The bottom descriptor must read “Professional Corporation”, not “Corp”, because of the registered legal entity.
04 · Wayfinder set aside
Right register, wrong audience
Dâmaris named the Wayfinder as ideal for a startup clientele. Because her preferred client is more traditional and higher-fee, the territory does not fit.
05 · Architect retained as comparison
Close to Counsel in posture, second on preference
Dâmaris read Architect and Counsel as adjacent territories. Counsel won on the colour direction and the editorial tone.
06 · Decision deadline
Friday lock-in, possibly tomorrow
Dâmaris committed to a written decision by 29 May, with the option to come back sooner. Both documents (strategy and design territories) go to her on WhatsApp tonight.
07 · Website timeline reset
Original month-one target slips by one week
Plan was Website done in month one of the pilot. Actual: one week and a half more after lock-in. Target completion: 10 June. Brand decision is the gate that controls it.
08 · Word template promised
Branded engagement-letter template after logo lock
Once the logo is locked, Adriano produces an editable Word template that carries the visual identity. Dâmaris keeps her own content per client; the template carries the style.

Operating note · tone alignment

The sophistication frame.

Dâmaris stated the working principle on the call: not every client is the right client. The brand has to do the screening at first impression. The Counsel territory does that work because it reads as considered and selective rather than aggressive or volume-coded. This frame carries into every downstream surface, including Website copy.
