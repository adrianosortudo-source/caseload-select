01 · Bottom Line

Two directories, four asks, ten minutes of your time

CaseLoad Select ran a full audit of the external citation graph for DRG Law on 20 May 2026. The audit narrowed an original list of eight directories down to two that are worth submitting this round. The submissions will be run by us. You provide four pieces of information once, then the work happens without you.

What is happening

Local search ranking for a Toronto solo practice depends on consistent business data across the directories that Google trusts. Your Law Society of Ontario record was aligned by you on 12 to 13 May 2026 and now reads correctly. The next step is to push that same record into the other directories that matter for Ontario, so that the citation graph agrees with the LSO and reinforces your local-search signal.

What we found during the audit

Three of the original directories on our shortlist (Martindale-Hubbell, Avvo, Justia) are now operationally US-only and add no value in Ontario. Two directories (Canadian Law List and Yellow Pages Canada) are the realistic free Canadian options. Two more (LawyerLocate, FindLaw Canada) are paid-only and have been deferred for a 60-day review.

The audit also surfaced two legacy "LexTransact Law" stub listings already in the directories, both showing a Markham address and contact details that do not match your current data. These need a yes-or-no decision from you in this round.

What we need from you

Four items, detailed in section 05: your year of call to the Ontario Bar, a preference on how verification emails should reach us, a 30-minute window in the next 5 to 7 business days for one short sales callback (two callbacks actually, one from each directory, but they can land in the same window), and a confirmation on whether LexTransact was your own corporation or a firm you worked at with others.

Why the original eight was cut to two

Martindale, Avvo, and Justia have all consolidated around US-only coverage. Martindale.ca is dead. Their search filters list only US states. Submitting an Ontario lawyer to any of them produces a US-targeted profile with near-zero Canadian local-SEO weight. LawyerLocate and FindLaw are paid-only with multi-year terms; they are worth considering at Day 60 if the free-tier submissions do not move the needle. That leaves Canadian Law List and Yellow Pages as the two free Canadian directories that actually carry weight for an Ontario solo practice.
