Act 02 · Strategy anchor
Positioning · Archetype
Strategy anchor · from chapters 1-6 of the brand book

The visual system must express the Authority Positioning.

Positioning
Legal clarity framed around where your business needs to go. DRG starts with the business goal, then frames the legal structure around the decision it needs to support.
Archetype
Sage Authority + Caregiver Warmth. Sage clarifies, structures, cites, explains, turning law into business decision-quality. Caregiver protects the owner from signing, buying, leasing, or restructuring without understanding.
Personality
Calm legal translator. Not aggressive fighter, not luxury advisor, not friendly generalist, not language-only community firm.
Target audience
SMB owners in Ontario with recurring corporate and real-estate needs. Referral-driven, calls before clicking, asks the referring contact for "the lawyer's name" before opening a browser.

Act 02 · Evaluation rules
How we judge
Evaluation rules · how the options should be judged

Six criteria. The winning territory must pass all six.

Rule 01

Does it express Sage first?

The brand needs to feel clarifying, structured, legally competent before it feels warm.

Rule 02

Does it keep warmth?

One-lawyer practice. The system must leave room for Damaris and personal accountability.

Rule 03

Does it show the decision?

Legal work starts with the business goal and ends with a clear next step. The doctrine made visible.

Rule 04

Does it avoid legal cliche?

No gavels, scales, columns, flags, handshake stock. Bay-Street burgundy and luxury-club gold banned.

Rule 05

Does it scale?

Must work in small places: emails, PDFs, forms, document headers, LinkedIn posts.

Rule 06

Does it feel ownable?

Specific enough for DRG to build recurring content and proof assets around it.

The middle dot · a moment of attention

A typographic mark that holds DRG and Law together in the wordmark, and recurs through every masthead, byline, and lockup. The dot is a held breath between two words, the same care a reader gives to a serious sentence before the next stress.

Set in oxblood at the wordmark's x-height. Never substituted for a comma or period; never used as a bullet. The mark reads as a moment of attention, not as decoration.

Counsel · Why these colours
5 colours · reasoning
Why these colours

A counselor's office, not a marketing site.

Oxblood
#6E2C2C
The colour of leatherbound spines, university crests, judicial seals. Says centuries of practice behind the desk. For the Sage half: legitimacy without flash. Rationed to the mark and structural accents so it never reads loud.
Brass
#B8956A
The colour of nameplates, lecterns, cufflinks. Carries quiet wealth and craft. For the Caregiver half: the warm metal a client touches at the door. Used as the dividing rule, never the headline.
Cream
#EFE9DD
The paper colour of high-stock stationery. Removes the white-screen glare and signals this is a document, not a feed. For the SMB owner mid-deal: reading-room calm.
Ink
#1A1410
Softer than pure black, closer to printed type. Holds long body text without fatiguing the eye on a phone or a printed brief. The text-stack default.
Paper
#FFFCF6
The warmest near-white. Document grounds, letterhead, brief covers. Same reading-room signal as cream, dialed up for legibility on long-form pages.
How the palette serves the strategy Oxblood plays the Sage role and is rationed. Brass plays the Caregiver role and stays quiet. Cream and paper carry the warmth so the surface never feels clinical. For an owner mid-deal, the palette says: your matter is being handled by someone who has done this before, on stationery.
Counsel · Why these fonts
Source Serif 4 throughout
Why these fonts

One reading serif. Every weight, every size.

Display + heads
Source Serif 4 · 700
Headlines, mastheads, document titles.
Legal clarity protects the decisions that build the business.
Adobe's modern reading serif. Carries the institutional weight of a serif without the dusty stiffness of Times or Garamond. Wide optical sizes mean the same family holds a 78px headline and 12px body. One face, calm authority.
Body
Source Serif 4 · 400
Long-form, briefs, correspondence.
The client gets the legal structure in writing, before they sign. Risks, price, timeline, next step.
Built specifically for long reading. The owner gets a real document to read, not a web page to scroll. Signals the firm puts things in writing, which is the operational clarity POD made visible.
Section labels
Source Serif 4 · 600 small caps
Eyebrows, masthead runners, table headers.
Section · Matter · Subject
Replaces the all-caps geometric eyebrow with something quieter. Small caps in a reading serif feel like a casebook running head. Reinforces the document-first identity without raising the volume.
How the type system serves the strategy Source Serif 4 across every weight and size collapses the type system to one family. Visual coherence becomes free; the system gets simpler to operate without losing range. For Damaris as a solo with no design team, the cost of running the brand drops to zero choices per piece.

How the palette serves the strategy The system runs on one structural dark and one signal accent. Three neutrals carry the rest. The owner sees a calm map with one moving dot. This is operational clarity rendered as colour: a complicated matter becomes a labeled route.

The client gets the legal structure in writing, before they sign. Risks, price, timeline, next step.
The most-tested screen-reading sans on the web. For long-form briefs and document bodies the owner will read on a phone, a desktop, and a PDF in the same week. Disappears under the words.

Metadata, matter IDs, dates, station markers.

How the type system serves the strategy Three faces, three sharply defined roles. The geometric sans for the way ahead, the reading sans for the substance, the mono for coordinates. The owner who looks at a brief or a website knows what to read first because the type system has already told them.

The terminal period · the closed decision

A single period that sits at the end of every headline, the end of the wordmark itself, and the end of every structured block. The period is the moment a matter closes: the brief signed, the lease executed, the decision behind the owner instead of ahead of them.

Set in moss green at the surrounding type's weight, in a colour the rest of the system never uses. The closure reads as deliberate, not as ordinary punctuation.

Wordmark: DRG Law.
Monogram: DRG.
Headline closure: ...business.
Section closer: end-of-block .
Footer mark: DRG Law .
Reserved colour: moss never elsewhere.
Practice · Why these colours
5 colours · reasoning
Why these colours

One structural dark. One chromatic accent. Three neutrals.

Ink
#0F1218
The colour of newspaper masthead type. Carries the editorial weight without the heaviness of pure black. Default for headlines and body. Reads as written by a serious operator.
Paper
#F7F4ED
Warm reading ground for documents. The cream cast removes screen-glare and signals a real document, not a webform. The owner reads a brief and recognises the surface as something serious.
Moss
#2A4A3A
The colour of leatherbound desk accessories, drafting-board cloth, library shelving. Carries Caregiver warmth and Sage stability at once. Owns the terminal period and the signature accent. The only chromatic note.
Slate
#5C6770
Neutral grey for secondary text and metadata. Recedes so the ink stays primary. The colour of fileroom labels and instrument metadata.
Warm Sand
#C8B89D
The lightest support tone. Tertiary chrome, rules, dividers. The neutral that keeps the system from going monochrome without ever competing with the moss accent.
How the palette serves the strategy The Practice runs on one structural dark, one chromatic accent, three neutrals. Pulled-back saturation lets the moss period do all the chromatic work. For the SMB owner, the system reads as the most modern law firm they have seen. For Damaris, the system reads as one accent colour means one decision per surface.
Practice · Why these fonts
Inter + JetBrains Mono
Why these fonts

Inter holds the document. Mono holds the data.

Display + heads
Inter · 700
Headlines, document titles, wordmark.
Legal clarity protects the decisions that build the business.
The default screen-reading sans of the modern web. Carries the editorial weight of newspaper headlines without referring to the past. The modern serious operator voice on every surface.
Body
Inter · 400
Long-form, brief body, correspondence.
The client gets the legal structure in writing, before they sign. Risks, price, timeline, next step.
Same family, lower weight. Built for screen reading at any size. The body face disappears so the words carry. The owner reads the matter, not the typography.
Metadata + labels
JetBrains Mono · 500
Matter numbers, dates, fees, eyebrows.
MATTER MTR-2026-0184 · 2026-05-27
Matter numbers, dates, fees, eyebrows. The mono treatment frames every piece of operational data with the visual cue of this is a record, this is exact.
How the type system serves the strategy Two families, three roles. Inter holds the document; mono holds the data. The owner gets an editorial brief on Inter and a deal sheet on mono and recognises both as the same firm. The type system itself is the operational clarity.
Practice · Discipline
5 rules per surface
Discipline · five rules that govern every Practice surface

Saturation. Grid. Balance. Deployment. Anti-trope.

Saturation ceiling
Moss alone exceeds the cap
Ink, paper, slate, warm sand all desaturated. Moss is the single signature.
Warm sand carries chrome work; moss reserved.
Spatial discipline
Strict baseline grid · asymmetric
8-point baseline grid. Body line-height 1.55-1.65.
Three-tier type scale with ≥1.5× line-height intervals.
Balance type
Asymmetric on grid
Rule-of-thirds anchoring for hero blocks.
Centred composition forbidden except for closing marks.
Signature deployment
Period closes every block
Wordmark + headline closure on every h1 / h2 + footer mark.
Period only at moments of closure. Never mid-sentence in moss.
Anti-trope list
What The Practice is NOT
Reject: Scandi-furniture moss. Tech-startup gradients. Centred hero with huge logo.
Mono decoration. Mono is for metadata only.

Close · Governance
Token freeze
Cross-territory discipline · protects whichever territory ships

Token freeze at lock. Lint-on-create for every new asset.

Solo operators are the most exposed to design-system drift. Six months from now there will be twenty client-facing assets, and three of them will be off-brand in ways the operator stopped seeing weeks ago. The moment the chosen territory is locked, the spec freezes; every subsequent asset gets a lint pass against the frozen spec before it ships.

01 · Freeze

Token list locked at selection

Hex codes, font sizes, weights, line-heights, spacing scale, glyph dims, signature placements. Stored at tokens.md.

02 · Lint

Every new asset audited

Before any client-facing asset ships, run a lint prompt comparing the asset to the token list and flagging deviations.

03 · Document

Prompt and output saved together

Every AI-assisted generation logged at prompts.md with timestamp and tool version. The prompt is the IP.
