Why this access matters

Local search is where most legal leads start the journey

Over 60 percent of prospective clients in the Toronto legal market run a Google search before they contact a firm. The Google Business Profile is what they find: the firm's name, address, phone, hours, photos, services, and the review stars. A poorly maintained or unverified profile loses ranking against firms with active profiles. Manager access lets CaseLoad Select keep the profile current, respond to reviews on the firm's behalf under your guidance, and run the local SEO optimisations that move ranking.

The role is added by email. You go to your Google Business Profile, add me as Manager, and I receive an invitation. Manager is the second-highest role tier; it permits all the operational work without giving up Owner rights. You stay Owner alongside me and can remove me from the same screen at any time.

What this access permits, what it does not

CaseLoad Select can
  • Edit business information (hours, services, attributes, description)
  • Add and manage photos
  • Respond to reviews and Q&A
  • Post updates, offers, and events
  • Manage GBP chat / messages (when enabled)
  • View profile insights and search performance
  • Configure messaging notifications
CaseLoad Select cannot
  • Add or remove other managers (Owner-only)
  • Transfer or delete the profile (Owner-only)
  • Access your personal Google account, Gmail, or Drive
  • See your Google Ads accounts unless explicitly granted
  • Modify your linked Google Business Profile website (if you use one) without your sign-off

The role being requested is Manager. The hierarchy is Primary Owner > Owner > Manager. Manager covers everything operational and editorial without the destructive privileges (delete, transfer, manage other roles) that should stay with you.

Reversible at any time

One click in the same Users screen removes my access. Profile data is unaffected — only my role disappears. Use removal any time you feel uncertain about the access. Nothing about this is permanent without your continued consent.

01
Step 1 · About 1 minute

Sign in at business.google.com

Open business.google.com in your browser and sign in with the Google account that owns the DRG Law business profile. If the firm has multiple profiles or you manage several businesses, the home screen shows them as cards; click into the DRG Law card. Then click Manage profile (or simply the profile name) to enter the management surface.

Already done?
If you already added me as a Manager from a previous request, that grant still applies — Google does not auto-expire Manager roles. On the form, just mark this section as "Done — access granted" and skip ahead. The next steps below are for first-time grants.
02
Step 2 · About 2 minutes

Open Settings, then Managers and owners

In the left sidebar of the Business Profile manager, click Settings. From the Settings menu, choose Managers and owners (sometimes labelled just "People" or "Users" depending on the GBP rollout in your account). The screen lists everyone with a role on this profile. Click the + Add user button at the top.

03
Step 3 · About 2 minutes

Add me by email and pick the Manager role

The Add user dialog opens. Enter my email, pick the Manager role from the role picker (not Owner — Manager is what we need), and click Invite. Google sends me an email with the invitation. I accept, sign in with my Google account, and the role activates within minutes. You will see "Adriano Domingues" appear in the Managers list with status Active.

What happens next

From access grant to a polished local search footprint

Once the role is granted, CaseLoad Select runs a profile audit within 48 hours and surfaces what is missing or out-of-date: hours, services, photos, attributes, and the categorisation that Google ranking relies on. Local SEO is the lowest-effort highest-impact work in the Capture pillar; profile completeness alone moves rank meaningfully for solo and small-firm listings in Toronto.

When you finish Steps 1 to 3

Send me a quick WhatsApp confirming the invitation is on its way. I will accept within the hour and start the profile audit that day.

A note on review responses

CaseLoad Select responds to reviews on the firm's behalf as part of standard service. Every response is brief, factual, and LSO-compliant — no outcome promises, no specialist or expert language, no disparagement of the reviewer regardless of the review content. You can opt to review and approve each response before it posts (slower but with full editorial control) or let CaseLoad Select post directly under guidelines you and I agree to upfront (faster, lower overhead, default for most firms). The choice is locked in during onboarding and revisable at any time.
