LinkedIn Company Page setup

What this guide does

Twenty minutes from your account, then I take it from there

LinkedIn requires that a Company Page be created from a personal account whose profile lists the company as a current employer and has a verified work email on the same domain. That gating cannot be delegated. After the page exists, anyone you invite as Super admin can run it without ever touching your password.

This guide walks you through the four screens you need to complete from your own LinkedIn login. None of it requires sharing credentials. None of it commits you to publishing anything. The page can sit empty for a week while we lock the brand and content strategy, then post one switches on.

The shape of the work

Step 1. Verify damaris@drglaw.ca on your personal LinkedIn account. Step 2. Add DRG Law as your current position. Step 3. Create the Company Page. Step 4. Add me as Super admin so I can publish from there.

What I do not need

Your LinkedIn password. Sharing personal credentials breaches LinkedIn's Terms of Service and would not even help, since Pages are a separate object that requires admin invitations regardless. Your account stays your account.

Time on your end

About twenty minutes total, including the email verification round trip. Easiest at a desktop, not phone.

What you will need

Access to the damaris@drglaw.ca inbox to confirm the verification code, and a square version of the DRG Law logo for the Page picture (we will provide if missing).

01
Step 1 · About 5 minutes

Verify damaris@drglaw.ca on your personal LinkedIn account

Open linkedin.com/psettings/email while logged in to your personal LinkedIn. Click Add email address, enter damaris@drglaw.ca, and complete the code-confirmation step that lands in your DRG inbox. This is the verification LinkedIn checks before allowing you to create a Company Page on the drglaw.ca domain.

Step 2 · About 5 minutes

Add DRG Law as your current position

Open your personal LinkedIn profile and click Add section › Core › Add position. Enter your title (Lawyer, Founder, or whatever you prefer), and in the Company or organization field, type DRG Law. The autocomplete will show existing companies. If DRG Law is not in the list yet, that is expected. Save the entry. We come back here in Step 3 after the Page is created, to attach you to it.

Step 4 · About 3 minutes

Add me as Super admin

From the new Page in admin view, click Settings in the left sidebar, then Manage admins. Click Add admin, type Adriano Domingues, pick my profile from the suggestions (linkedin.com/in/adriano-domingues), and select Super admin as the role. Save.

What happens next

Page created, then quiet, then live

The four steps stand the channel up structurally. After that, the Page sits dormant for one to two weeks while brand and content strategy land. Once those are approved, the channel turns on and the publishing cadence begins.

Day 0 · This week

Steps 1 to 4 complete

Page exists at linkedin.com/company/drg-law. Damaris is owner, Adriano is Super admin. Page is empty. No posts.

Days 1 to 5

Page populated

Logo, About copy, tagline, cover image, founded year, location, specialties, call-to-action button. Still no posts.

Days 5 to 14

Brand and content strategy approved

Brand identity locked. Content pillars and publishing cadence confirmed by you. Editorial calendar drafted for first 30 days.

Day 15 onward

Posting activates

First post goes live. Publishing on the agreed cadence, monitoring engagement, adjusting against the metrics that matter for inflow.

When you finish Steps 1 to 4

Send me a one-line WhatsApp confirming the Page is live and I am added as admin. Include the linkedin.com/company/* URL. From there I take over.

Compliance note

All public content on the Page will follow LSO Rule 4.2-1. No outcome promises, no specialist or expert language, no unverifiable superlatives. Final approval on every post stays with you until we agree on a tighter pre-approval rhythm.
