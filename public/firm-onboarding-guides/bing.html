Setup walkthrough · Bing Places for Business

Add CaseLoad Select as Manager on your Bing Places listing

About fifteen minutes. Bing powers Microsoft Copilot, DuckDuckGo, and increasingly ChatGPT-search results. Listing your firm on Bing Places makes it discoverable to clients searching from those surfaces, not just Bing search itself. Same pattern as the GBP setup: you stay as Owner; CaseLoad Select takes Manager access so we can keep the listing fresh and respond to inquiries.

Time~15 minutes
You'll needMicrosoft account + your firm address + a phone for verification
Sign-in URLbusiness.bing.com

Why we ask for Manager access

Manager access lets us update hours, photos, services, and respond to listing inquiries on your behalf. It does not transfer ownership. You stay as Owner, you can revoke our access at any time, and only you can delete the listing. Same arrangement as the Google Business Profile setup.

Five steps. Have your phone nearby for verification.

1

Sign in at business.bing.com

Use a Microsoft account. Personal Outlook or Hotmail works, but a firm-domain Microsoft account is preferred for long-term ownership clarity. If your firm uses Microsoft 365 for email, sign in with that.

If you don't have a Microsoft account yet, click "Create account" on the sign-in page. Takes two minutes; no payment details required.

[Screenshot · business.bing.com sign-in screen]
2

Claim or add your business

After signing in, you'll land on the Bing Places dashboard. Two paths:

  • If Bing already has a listing for your firm (auto-generated from public sources), click Claim next to it. Bing matches by business name + address.
  • If no listing exists yet, click Add a new business and fill in the firm details: legal business name, primary category (Lawyer / Law Firm), address, phone, website, hours.
[Screenshot · Claim vs Add a new business]
3

Verify the listing

Bing offers three verification methods. Pick the fastest for your situation:

  • Phone call or text to the business phone number on the listing. Instant.
  • Postcard sent to the registered business address. 5-7 business days.
  • Video verification, where Bing asks you to record a short clip showing the business location and signage. Available for some categories.

Verification is required before the listing goes live or before you can add a Manager.

[Screenshot · Verification method selection]
4

Settings → Manage Users → Add user

Once verified, open Settings from the left sidebar, then Manage Users. Click Add user.

  • Email: adriano@caseloadselect.ca
  • Role: Manager (not Owner)

Manager access lets us edit the listing, respond to inquiries, and post updates. It does not let us delete the listing or transfer ownership.

[Screenshot · Manage Users panel with Add user button]
5

Send the invitation

Bing emails the invitation to adriano@caseloadselect.ca. We accept the invite from our side within the business day. You're done.

From here on, you only touch Bing Places if you want to publish something specific. Routine updates (new photos, hours changes, service additions) go through us.

After you finish

Mark the status as Done in your onboarding form

Go back to your onboarding form and set the Bing Places Manager status to Done: access granted. If you hit a blocker (account issue, verification failed, role unavailable), set it to Blocked and write a line in the notes. We'll respond within the business day.
